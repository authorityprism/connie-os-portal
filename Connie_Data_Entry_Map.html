Which Paperwork Loads

Different state = different forms. The property address tells Dotloop which set to use.

Rhode Island

RIAR FORMS · VIA TRANSACTION DESK
  • Single-Family Purchase & Sales Agreement
  • Professional Services Fee (new Aug 2024 — NAR settlement)
  • Exclusive Buyer Representation Agreement
  • Lead Paint Disclosure
  • Cesspool Replacement Disclosure (RI-specific)
  • Agency Disclosure
  • Property Condition Disclosure
  • Escalation Addendum
  • Sale of Buyer's Property (Hubbard) (updated Oct 2025)
  • Release of P&S and Escrowed Funds

RIAR sends updates directly to Dotloop. Templates stay current automatically.

Massachusetts

MAR FORMS · MASSFORMS LIBRARY
  • Form 501 — Contract to Purchase (updated Feb 2025)
  • Standard Purchase & Sale Agreement
  • Mandatory Licensee-Consumer Disclosure (MA state requirement)
  • Lead Paint Notification & Certification
  • Home Inspectors Facts brochure
  • Form 311 — Broker-to-Broker Compensation (accept/reject + arbitration)
  • Form 520A — Escalation Provisions
  • Title V Septic Compliance (MA-only requirement)
  • Form 706 — Termination of Buyer Broker

MA removed "net" commission in 2025. Now gross percentage only on all forms.

Connecticut

CTR FORMS · ~100 IN LIBRARY
  • Real Estate Purchase Contract (REPC) (updated June 2025)
  • Residential Property Condition Report (CGS §20-327b — $300 credit if missing)
  • Exclusive Right to Sell Agreement (updated Oct 2025)
  • Exclusive Right to Represent Buyer
  • Dual Agency Consent Agreement
  • HUD Lead-Based Paint Disclosure
  • Attorney Approval Rider (CT uses attorney review)
  • Unsatisfactory Inspection Notification
  • Septic/Well Rider
  • Foundation Condition Report (CT crumbling foundation issue)

Co-branded CTR/GHAR forms. 8 authorized vendors including Dotloop.

(RIAR = Rhode Island Association of Realtors. MAR = Massachusetts Association of Realtors. CTR = CT Realtors. Each group writes the official forms for their state. Dotloop keeps them updated automatically — when the association revises a form, your templates refresh without you doing anything.)

WHAT THIS LOOKS LIKE IN REAL LIFE

Six months from now, your system says:

"Text TJ Wright — his daughter's birthday is next Tuesday. He likes gardening. He found you through rollerskating. Draft: 'Hey TJ! Hope the garden is coming along this spring 🌱 Autumn has a birthday coming up — tell her happy birthday from us!'"

That's not a cold call. That's a relationship. And the system wrote the text for you.

One form. Two systems. Zero wasted effort.

Your intake form does double duty. 8 fields go to Dotloop to fill out your contracts so you never type the same name twice. 7 fields go to your relationship engine so you never forget a birthday, a hobby, or a kid's name.

And when the deal closes, the closing date goes back in as a home anniversary. Next year, the system reminds you. The year after that too. And every year after.

That's how you go from "I sold them a house" to "they'll never use anyone else."
