How Your Deals Flow

From "I'm ready" to "SOLD" — here's exactly how your contacts, your forms, and your system talk to each other so you never type the same thing twice.

1

Someone Says "I'm Ready"

This is where a name becomes a deal

A contact in your system is ready to move

They filled out your form. You've been nurturing them. They called you back. However it happened — it's go time.

You mark them as a Deal in FUB

Inside Follow Up Boss (your contact database — FUB), you click on their name and create a Deal. You pick whether they're buying or selling. You add the property address if you have it.

This one click is the domino. Everything after this can happen automatically.

Is FUB connected to Dotloop?

There's a button in FUB that connects it to Dotloop (Admin → Integrations → Dotloop). You only set this up once. If it's connected, the magic starts. If not, Aly is typing everything by hand.

✓ CONNECTED

Loop creates itself

Their name, email, phone, and address flow straight into Dotloop. (A "loop" is Dotloop's word for a deal folder — all the paperwork for one transaction lives inside it.) No typing. No copy-paste.

✗ NOT CONNECTED

Manual entry

Someone opens Dotloop, creates a new loop, and types in the name, email, phone, address, and role by hand. Same info that's already in FUB. This is where time dies.

both roads lead to the same place — a new loop
2

Which State, Which Forms

You work in three states — each one has different paperwork

Where is the property?

You're licensed in Rhode Island, Massachusetts, and Connecticut. Each state has its own set of contracts, disclosures, and legal forms. The property address tells Dotloop which forms to load.

🏠 RHODE ISLAND

RIAR Forms

Your home base. (RIAR = Rhode Island Association of Realtors — they write the official forms. You access them through Transaction Desk on the State-Wide MLS gateway.)

• Single-Family Purchase & Sales Agreement
• Professional Services Fee section (new since Aug 2024 — the NAR settlement changed how buyer broker compensation works.)
• Exclusive Buyer Representation Agreement
• Lead Paint Disclosure
• Cesspool Replacement Disclosure (RI-specific)
• Agency Disclosure
• Property Condition Disclosure
• Escalation Addendum
• Sale of Buyer's Property Addendum (Hubbard clause — updated Oct 2025)
• Release of P&S and Escrowed Funds

RIAR sends form updates directly to Dotloop. When they revise a form, your templates update automatically.

🏠 MASSACHUSETTS

MAR Forms

Different state, different rules. (MAR = Massachusetts Association of Realtors. Forms are in the MassForms library.)

• Form 501 — Contract to Purchase (updated Feb 2025)
• Standard Purchase & Sale Agreement
• Mandatory Licensee-Consumer Relationship Disclosure
• Lead Paint Notification & Certification
• Home Inspectors Facts for Consumers brochure
• Form 311 — Broker-to-Broker Compensation (updated 2025)
• Form 706 — Termination of Buyer Broker Agreement
• Escalation Provisions (Form 520A)
• Title V Septic Compliance (MA requires septic inspections)

MA removed "net" as a commission option in 2025. Commission is now gross percentage only.

🏠 CONNECTICUT

CTR Forms

Nearly 100 forms in this library. (CTR = CT Realtors. 8 authorized form vendors including Dotloop.)

• Real Estate Purchase Contract (REPC) (updated June 2025)
• Residential Property Condition Report (RPCR)
• Exclusive Right to Sell Agreement (updated Oct 2025)
• Exclusive Right to Represent Buyer Agreement
• Dual Agency Consent Agreement
• HUD Lead-Based Paint Disclosure — Sales
• Attorney Approval Rider (CT uses attorney review)
• Unsatisfactory Inspection Notification
• Septic/Well Rider
• Foundation Condition Report (CT crumbling foundation crisis)

CT forms are co-branded between CTR and GHAR. Copyrighted — can't be edited or posted.

right forms loaded — now fill them in
3

The Forms Fill Themselves

Everything you already collected does the heavy lifting

Your intake form already has most of this info

Remember your Connect With Connie form? That beautiful thing that asks for their name, birthday, kids' names, hobby, lender, towns they like?

All of that is sitting in FUB right now. When Dotloop creates the loop, it pulls from the same data. The buyer's name fills into the contract. Their address fills into the disclosures. The lender info fills into the financials section.

You captured it once. It works everywhere.

Buyer or seller?

This determines which side of the paperwork gets filled. Buyers get one set of fields. Sellers get another. But the contact info flows into both.

BUYING

Buyer fields populate

Name, email, phone → People section (the contacts list inside the loop). Lender + approval amount → Financials. Towns of interest → loop notes. You're tagged as Buying Agent.

SELLING

Seller fields populate

Name, email, phone → People section. Property address → every document that needs it (Purchase & Sales, disclosures, listing agreement). You're tagged as Listing Agent. MLS number links when you go active.

loop is loaded — people and property are in
4

Check for Gaps

Some things need your eye before they go out

Dotloop autofill runs across all your documents

Every form in the loop that has labeled fields (smart fields that know what info to pull) fills in automatically. Buyer name? Done. Property address? Done. Your brokerage info? Already there from your Dotloop profile.

✓ ALL FILLED

Ready for your review

Everything looks right. Give it a quick scan, then share with your clients for signing.

⚠ SOME GAPS

You or Aly fill the rest

Missing lender details, additional party names (co-buyers, attorneys, inspectors), or deal-specific terms. Quick manual fill — takes a few minutes, not an hour.

documents are complete
5

Sign and Submit

Clients sign on their phone. You submit for review.

Share the documents

You tap "Share" and choose what each person can do. Clients can sign and fill out their parts. Co-agents can edit. Everyone works from their phone, tablet, or laptop.

No printer. No scanner. No driving to the office to sign a piece of paper.

✓ ALL SIGNED

Submit for compliance

You hit "Submit for Review." Here Realty's office gets notified. (Compliance = making sure all the required documents are in order before the deal moves forward.) They can see your loop the moment you create it.

✗ MISSING SIGS

Chase the signature

Dotloop shows you exactly who hasn't signed yet — and whether they've even opened the document. That's your follow-up cue. Send a reminder. Get it done.

paperwork complete — deal moves to close
6

Close → Circle Back

The deal ends. The relationship doesn't.

Transaction closes. You mark it "SOLD."

Loop status changes. All documents are stored forever for compliance. Done.

But here's the part most realtors miss:

The closing date goes back into FUB

That date is now their home anniversary. One year from today, your system says:

"Reach out to Maria Santos — it's her one-year homeowner anniversary. She bought at 42 Waterview Dr. Her daughter Sofia turns 6 next month."

The birthday you captured on your intake form. The closing date from Dotloop. The kid's birthday from that first conversation. It all comes back around.

Deal done. Relationship forever.

They move from active deal back into your sphere. The system keeps nurturing them. The next deal starts the same way — except now you know even more about them.

Here's the big picture

Someone fills out your form. You captured 17 pieces of information about them — their name, their kids, their birthday, their hobby, their lender, what towns they like.

8 of those fields flow straight into Dotloop when it's time to do the deal. No retyping. The other 9 fields — birthday, hobby, kids' names, how they found you — those stay in the relationship engine. They power the texts, the birthday cards, the "I remembered your daughter's recital" moments.

One form. Two systems. Zero wasted effort.

And when the deal closes, that date feeds back in. Next year, the system says "it's their anniversary." That's how you turn one transaction into a lifetime relationship.
